# Product Review

## Sharpest Critique

The phrase "AI Water Meter" is catchy, but a meter implies direct measurement. A browser extension cannot know the exact model route, GPU, data center, cooling system, batching behavior, cache hit rate, PUE, WUE, or grid mix for a private provider request.

The product earns trust only if the UI says what it really is: a local, source-backed estimate from visible chat text.

## Likely User Objections

- "There is no way the browser can see OpenAI's data-center water use."
- "Why does this show precise decimals if the model and data center are unknown?"
- "Is this reading or uploading my prompts?"
- "Does this work for long reasoning agents the same way it works for a short chat?"
- "Is this climate accounting or just awareness?"

## Product Response

- Lead with "Estimated footprint range" instead of exact water claims.
- Show direct water, indirect grid water, energy, and carbon separately.
- Keep "Not provider telemetry" visible in the sidebar and popup.
- Keep prompts and responses local; store only aggregate totals.
- Document sources, accounting boundaries, and caveats.
- Add estimator profiles for median text, long-context, reasoning-heavy, image, and video workflows before claiming broader precision.

## Positioning

AI Water Meter should position itself as an awareness and transparency tool, not a formal emissions-accounting system.

Best promise:

> See a local, source-backed estimate of the water, energy, and carbon impact of your AI chats, without sending your prompts anywhere.
